The first burger draft was rejected because its ingredients floated apart; it is not included in the application.

Add-on transparency was prepared with a local, one-off chroma-key preprocessing step using Pillow. That build-time tooling is not part of the app. Final PNG SHA-256 checksums are recorded in `ASSET_CHECKSUMS.md`.

The six `*_mask.png` resources are deterministic merchant-style author masks derived from the approved base photographs and generated by `Tools/GenerateAuthorMasks.swift`. The masks resolve touches and localize the reconstruction animation. The finished state is a complete reviewed destination photograph so the prototype cannot expose seams from a partial composite. They contain no third-party content and can be regenerated locally.

## Restaurant-flow visual system (19 July 2026)

The restaurant catalog, exploded editor, and matched preview assets added on 19 July 2026 were also created with OpenAI's built-in image generation tool. They are original prototype assets; no Zomato, Subway, restaurant, delivery-app, or stock-product screenshots were copied.

|---|---|---|

The sprite sheets were only intermediate build artifacts. Final transparent PNGs are the files bundled in `DishEdit/Resources`. The master prompts explicitly prohibited hands, text, logos, packaging, utensils crossing the food, or branded presentation.

The current prototype describes its 5.2-second reconstruction as a visual preview. The animation is stage presentation around a reviewed local destination photograph; it does not claim that the simulator generated those pixels during that run. The order itself remains structured catalog state.
